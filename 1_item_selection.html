Introduction


In this "lie detection" experiment, we simulate a situation in which someone is trying to hide his or her identity (as e.g. a spy or a terrorist), but we suspect that we know some true personal details of this person. You will play this person, and will be simulating either a "guilty" or an "innocent" case.

Please read the instructions carefully.




The following details will provide material for the eventual test, so please answer honestly.

Your country of origin:

Your birthday:

Your favorite animal (among the presented options):


Please look through the list below, and select, in each category (countries, dates, or animals), maximum two items that seem personally important or meaningful for you, or that seem in any way different from the rest of the items: for example, a country that you have often visited, or an animal that you especially admire, etc. (These items will then not be used in the test.) If there are no such items in a given category, just select "None". You can select (or deselect) by clicking on the items.








Now comes the lie detection test. Of course, we actually know your details (country of origin, etc.), since you just selected them, but, during the entire following test, for the sake of the simulation, please act as if we did not know that these details are yours, and try to pretend that you do not recognize them.

Important: it is possible that you will really not see your own details in the test. This simply means that you are in the "innocent" condition, simulating an innocent suspect.

When you are ready to start the test, call the experiment leader.
